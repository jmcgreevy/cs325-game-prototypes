A Murder Most Fowl

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    It's a murder mystery starring chickens. Other than that naturally being an easy way to make a punny title, I think conceptually its funny enough to get a chuckle out of some people. And I thought it was pretty clever that the culprit was actually the unassuming dog.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    As per the mandated variety, I decided to make a text-based game since my other games didn't have such mechanics. Unfortunately there's not too much that's interesting about just a text game.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype?
    I originally wanted to play around with the text options but I was having trouble getting anything to work. As it stands now, all the text is actually part of the backgrounds when the states are loaded in. If i was able to get text working properly, that could've opened the doors for randomization of clues and murder suspects (if I had enough chicken art)
  • Prototype Assets: All of the art I made myself
    The music I used (called Possession) is from here:
  • https://www.purple-planet.com/creepy
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Its similar to any other text-based point and click game, although I wrote all the code myself based off of the state examples.
  • High Concept: A one-sentence summary of your game.
    As Detective Cluck, your job is to pick amongst the suspects from your list of clues, in order to make sure evil-dooers are punished.
  • Theme: State which of the Storymatic themes you used.
    I used the "chicken" storymatic theme. If I had more time to write, I could've also tied it in with "revenge."
  • Mandated Variety: State which input, randomness, genre, and play style you used in this prototype.
    The genre and input is a point and click text-game. The game itself is a whodunnit but because there's no randomness, it is missing replay value.
  • Prototype Goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating?
    This game is completely controlled from the mouse, with no other imputs. Unlike my other games that used WASD or the arrow keys.
  • Player Experience Goals: What experience do you want players to have when playing your game?
    While its only a simple text game, I think players would have a chuckle given my dialogue and the reasonings behind each suspect.
  • Gameplay: In order to make sure the right suspect pays for their crimes, you must use deductive reasoning in order to identify who was the murderer.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    The game is pretty forgiving and there aren't a lot of options, so anyone would be able to win given enough time.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: You are Detective Cluck, a chicken who solves murder mysteries. It's your job to make sure the right chicken gets sent to jail.
  • Target Audience: People who like "whodunnit" mystery games. Or just puzzles in general.
  • Play Time: How long does your game take to play?
    The game is pretty short, even if you pick all the wrong options. From start to finish, its about 1-2 minutes depending on how fast you can read.
